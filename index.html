F.axis is not another social media. It is the social media you control — because we are fully transparent about how the algorithm works.

F.axis shows you where you sit in the information landscape, gives you tools to explore outside your bubble, and explains every post it shows you — no black box, no hidden algorithm.
F.axis helps brands reach the right audiences ethically and transparently — connecting with users who are genuinely open to your message, inside and beyond their usual content clusters.
Have you ever noticed this?
🤔
Why do you keep seeing the same type of content?
The more you scroll, the more the feed shows you what you already agree with. Not because it's the best content — because it keeps you engaged longer.
😤
Why does everything feel so polarised?
Outrage and fear keep people scrolling. The algorithm knows this. Division is not a side effect — for many platforms it's the engine.
🕵️
Why can't you see how the feed decides what you see?
Every major platform uses a black-box algorithm. You have no way to understand it, let alone change it. F.axis is built to be the opposite of that.
The advertising problem no one talks about
🎯
You only reach people who already know you
Algorithmic targeting places your brand inside existing content clusters — the same people, the same conversations, over and over. Your next customers are in clusters you've never touched.
😒
Users distrust ads they didn't ask for
Injected ads — placed without user consent in a feed they can't control — generate resentment. Low trust means low conversion, regardless of how good your creative is.
📦
No platform shows you how clusters connect to your brand
Understanding which information communities are adjacent to your audience — and which are open to discovery — is impossible on current platforms. F.axis makes this visible.

How F.axis works for brands
Two ways to reach new audiences — both transparent:
🎯
Within-cluster targeting
Reach users in content clusters that match your brand. F.axis verifies that users in that cluster are genuinely open to your category — because they chose to accept ads there.
Example: a cloud infrastructure brand reaching users in the Tech·AI cluster who have opted in to infrastructure ads.
🌉
Bridge ads — find new audiences
Place your brand at the crossing point between two clusters. Reach people who haven't discovered your category yet but are moving toward it — the highest-value growth audience.
Example: a sustainability brand reaching Tech users who are beginning to explore Climate content.
The opt-in model means users chose to see your category. You're not interrupting them — you're meeting them where they already want to go. That's why conversion quality on F.axis is fundamentally different.
See it from your users' side
The best way to understand F.axis as a platform partner is to experience it as a user first. Connect your feed and explore the demo — you'll see exactly how your audience experiences the content map, the echo score, and the ad section.
In the Ads tab you can see how bridge ads appear to users, how the "why this ad?" explanation works, and how opt-in controls are presented. This is what your brand campaigns will look like.

Content Affiliation Graph
How F.axis builds your personal map from scratch
▼

A content affiliation graph is a weighted graph G = (V, E, w) where each node v ∈ V is a content cluster, and an edge (u,v) ∈ E exists when you co-read topics u and v within a session window τ.

G = (V, E, w)
E = {(u,v) : co-read count ≥ θ = 3}
w(u,v) = co-read frequency / total sessions

Your graph: |V|=4 |E|=5
density = 2|E|/|V|(|V|−1) = 0.83

Structural Holes
The gaps in your map — and why they matter
2
holes found
▼

A structural hole is a gap between two clusters that are not directly connected in your map. Burt (1992) showed that bridging structural holes is where the most novel information comes from.

Constraint C(v) = Σ_u [p(v,u) + Σ_q p(v,q)·p(q,u)]²

p(v,u) = w(v,u) / Σ_j w(v,j) (proportion of time on edge)
C ∈ [0,1]: 0 = bridge position 1 = fully embedded

Your score: C = 0.74 — high embedding
Holes found: Economy↔Politics, Climate↔Politics
Your structural holes
Economy ↔ Politics
No direct connection. You read economic policy only through a Tech lens. A bridge post here would directly connect your macro understanding to political context.
Climate ↔ Politics
No direct connection despite high real-world overlap. F.axis bridge content is actively targeting this gap.
💡The Bridge dial specifically targets posts that would add a new edge to your graph — reducing your constraint score.

Betweenness Centrality
Which cluster is your information bottleneck
0.83
Tech·AI
▼

How often does a cluster appear on the shortest path between two other clusters? High betweenness = bottleneck. This is the metric most directly tied to echo chambers — all information passing through one node is a textbook filter bubble.

C_B(v) = Σ σ(s,t|v) / σ(s,t)

σ(s,t) = shortest paths from s to t
σ(s,t|v) = those paths passing through v

C_B = 5/6 = 0.83 ← your bottleneck

Eigenvector Centrality
Are your sources influential or just echo sources?
0.58
Tech·AI
▼

Not just how many connections, but how well-connected those connections are. Like Google PageRank — a link from a high-authority source counts more than ten from isolated echo chambers. Below 0.5 often signals an echo cluster.

C_E(v) = (1/λ) Σ C_E(u)

λ = largest eigenvalue of adjacency matrix A
Ax = λx (solved iteratively ~10 passes)

Katz Centrality
Your indirect reach — even via distant clusters
0.71
Tech·AI
▼

Like eigenvector centrality but counts all paths — including long indirect ones — with a penalty (α) that shrinks with distance. This is why F.axis can recommend content 3 hops away and still explain its relevance to you.

C_K(v) = Σk≥1 Σu α^k (A^k)_vu

α = 0.1 (F.axis attenuation factor)
Matrix form: (I − αA)^−1 · 1 − 1

Tech·AI Katz: 0.71 — broad indirect reach
Politics Katz: 0.29 — weak, distant signal
